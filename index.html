About Me

Hello! My name is Evan Sciancalepore, and I am a junior at Loyola Marymount University majoring in CS. I grew up in upstate New York, went to Tufts University in Boston for two years, and then transferred to LMU in Los Angeles in Fall 2021.

I've been interested in computers since I was a kidâ€”downloading random programs to my Mom's computer and setting up Minecraft servers for my friends. It wasn't until I got to college, however, that I realized I wanted to make a career out of it. My Intro to Computer Science class (COMP 11) at Tufts connected me with like-minded people and inspired me to pursue software engineering.

Since then, I've been taking every opportunity to learn and expand my skillset. I have experience in front-end development from my work with the Textbook Exchange Network, a website that aims to alleviate the burden of high textbook prices by providing a platform for students to efficiently buy and sell books.

This past summer (Summer 2021), I worked for Markit as a software engineering intern. Markit is a social networking app founded by Tufts alum Jonathan Chang that aims to make it easier than ever to plan events and connect with your friends. I helped develop new features alongside other members of the software team. Check out the app here!

In my free time, I enjoy hanging out at LA beaches, listening to music (Mac Miller, Frank Ocean, Tyler, the Creator and more), hiking (especially back home in the Adirondacks), and playing with my one-year-old puppy.

Thanks for reading, hopefully we can get in contact!
